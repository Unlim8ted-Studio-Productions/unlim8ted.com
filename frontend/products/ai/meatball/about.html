About the Meatball AI

A small browser AI made from fragments

The Meatball does not need a huge live AI model running on a server. Instead, it uses small browser-friendly models to understand the question, pick the right topic, choose useful answer fragments, and stitch them together.

The Simple Idea
The Meatball is not trying to invent every answer from scratch. It chooses from prepared pieces of knowledge.

1. Understand the question

It figures out what the user is asking about, especially when the question is vague or depends on the previous message.

2. Pick the right topic

It sends the question to the correct small model, like Unlim8ted projects, products, science, movies, or fallback.

3. Build the answer

It selects useful text fragments, joins them in order, and cleans up the final response.

How a Message Moves Through the System
History is used only to clarify the question. The answer system receives a clean standalone question.
User says
“does the company have clothes?”
This is vague because “the company” depends on what the user was just talking about.

Rewrite the question

The system replaces the vague phrase with the real subject.

after:
